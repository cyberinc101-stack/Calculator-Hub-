How to Calculate Percentages

A percentage is a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. The word "percent" means "per hundred" — 25% means 25 out of every 100.

The Core Percentage Formulas

  • X% of Y: Result = (X ÷ 100) × Y
  • X is what % of Y: Percentage = (X ÷ Y) × 100
  • Percentage change: ((New − Old) ÷ Old) × 100
  • Percentage difference: (|A − B| ÷ ((A + B) ÷ 2)) × 100
  • Reverse percentage (increase): Original = Final ÷ (1 + rate)
  • Reverse percentage (decrease): Original = Final ÷ (1 − rate)

Percentage Change vs Percentage Difference

These are often confused. Percentage change has a direction — you're measuring from a specific starting point. It's asymmetric: going from 50 to 100 is +100%, but going from 100 to 50 is −50%. Percentage difference has no direction — it symmetrically compares two values using their average as the base.

What is a reverse percentage?

A reverse (or inverse) percentage finds the original value before a percentage increase or decrease was applied. If a TV costs $299 after a 15% discount, the original price was $299 ÷ 0.85 = $351.76. A common mistake is to simply add 15% back — that gives the wrong answer because the percentage was taken from the original, larger value, not the discounted price.

How do I calculate a percentage increase?

Subtract the old value from the new value, divide by the old value, then multiply by 100. Example: sales grew from $80,000 to $104,000 — increase = (104,000 − 80,000) ÷ 80,000 × 100 = 30%. Use the % Change mode above for this.

What's a quick mental math trick for percentages?

Swap the numbers: 4% of 75 is the same as 75% of 4 = 3. This works because multiplication is commutative. Also: 10% is always easy (move the decimal left one place), then scale: 15% = 10% + 5%, and 5% = half of 10%.
