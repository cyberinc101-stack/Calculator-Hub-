How to Use the Auto Loan Calculator

Enter the vehicle price, any down payment or trade-in value, your sales tax rate, and the loan's APR and term. The calculator rolls sales tax and dealer fees into the amount financed by default — the most common real-world scenario — and shows your monthly payment, total interest, true APR, and a full amortisation schedule. Add an extra monthly payment to see how much interest you'd save.

Auto Loan vs. Personal Loan

Auto loans are secured against the vehicle itself, which is why their rates are typically lower than unsecured personal loans. They also involve extra variables a generic loan calculator doesn't account for — trade-in value, sales tax, and dealer fees — all of which affect the actual amount you finance. If you're comparing a vehicle purchase against a personal loan or just want a simpler payment estimate without these vehicle-specific factors, the general Loan Calculator covers personal, student, and other loan types.

Typical Auto Loan Interest Rates (2024–2025)

  • New car, excellent credit (720+): 5%–7%
  • New car, average credit (660–719): 7%–10%
  • Used car, excellent credit (720+): 6%–9%
  • Used car, average credit (660–719): 9%–13%
  • Subprime credit (below 600): 14%–21%+

How Much Should My Car Payment Be?

A common budgeting guideline is the 20/4/10 rule: put at least 20% down, finance for no more than 4 years, and keep total monthly vehicle costs (payment, insurance, fuel) under 10% of your gross monthly income. It's a guideline, not a hard rule, but it's a useful sanity check against payments that look affordable today but stretch your budget over a 6- or 7-year term.

What's a good APR for a car loan?

As of 2024–2025, well-qualified buyers (credit score 720+) typically see new car APRs of 5%–7% and used car APRs of 6%–9%. Below a 660 credit score, rates often climb to 12% or higher, and subprime borrowers can see APRs above 20%.

Should I include sales tax in my car loan calculation?

Most buyers finance sales tax as part of the loan rather than paying it upfront. This calculator rolls sales tax into the amount financed by default, which is the most common real-world scenario, but you can set the tax rate to 0% if you plan to pay it separately.

How does a down payment affect my car loan?

A larger down payment reduces the amount financed, which lowers your monthly payment and total interest paid. It also reduces the risk of being "upside down" (owing more than the car is worth) since vehicles depreciate quickly in the first few years.

What's the difference between a car loan and a lease?

With a car loan, you're financing the full purchase price and own the vehicle once it's paid off. With a lease, you're effectively paying for the vehicle's depreciation over a fixed term and returning it at the end, typically with lower monthly payments but no ownership equity.
